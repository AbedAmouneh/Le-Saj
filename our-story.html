The Story Behind Les Saj

From a shared love of food to Winnipeg's most beloved Middle Eastern restaurant — here's how it all began.

Our Beginning

How It All Started

Les Saj founders Mohamad, Rochelle, and Adam share one common trait — they're all true foodies. They love exploring new culinary experiences, cooking, and discovering new restaurants.

When they moved to Winnipeg, they noticed a growing demand for authentic Middle Eastern food. They saw how much Winnipeggers appreciate and enjoy Mediterranean cuisine. Their passion to create delicious, traditional flavours and share them with others led them to open Les Saj Restaurant on April 1, 2017 — their first location at 1038 St. James Street in Winnipeg.

The Name

What Does "Saj" Mean?

The name "Les Saj" is inspired by the word "saj" — a dome-shaped, convex metal griddle commonly used in the Middle East and Mediterranean to bake traditional flatbread.

At every Les Saj location, you can watch our chefs bake fresh saj bread right in our open kitchen — thin, warm, perfectly flexible, and the foundation of every wrap we serve.

This bread isn't just an ingredient. It's the heart of our identity — representing authenticity, craftsmanship, and our commitment to doing things the real way.
